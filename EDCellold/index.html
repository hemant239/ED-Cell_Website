Entrepreneurship Development Cell MNIT Jaipur

ED Cell has been set up in the Deptment of Management Studies, Malaviya National Institute of Technology, Jaipur. The Cell is sponsoured by Department of Science and Technology, Government of India to act as an institutional mechanism for providing various services including information to budiing S&T entrepreneur. Objective of ED Cell is to motivate students and provide necessary guidance and assitance to become sucessful entrepreneur.

Demystifying India's Start up Policies

The nation's starting to see a massive growth in start-ups with so many budding entrepreneurs who are still students. Now, quite recently, the Prime Minister of India, Narendra Modi launched the Start-up India, Stand India campaign to support start-ups and to provide all basic necessities to them and also making the government norms easy, so that they could only focus on their business development exclusively. So, the Entrepreneurship Development Cell of Malaviya National Institute of Technology conducted Start-up India Policies on 11th March 2016 in MNIT to add on to this thought, shedding light on the various policies of Start-up India. Paresh Gupta, a successful entrepreneur himself, conducted the lecture, parting knowledge to the students about the various policies, the pros and cons of the same and how to go about them. A lot was learnt at the end of the lecture and the attendees most definitely did leave the hall with a lot more clarity regarding the various policies surrounding start-ups.

Startup Saturday, Husband and Wife Cofounders

With the start-up community growing profusely in India; one of the most crucial ingredients in the success of start-ups is a good team. And what better than the team of life partners?
The March edition of Start-up Saturday, on 12th March 2016, held in Malaviya National Institute of Technology, Jaipur - an initiative of the Entrepreneurship Development Cell of MNIT in collaboration with Headstart - saw the pitches of some of the successful start-ups that are founded by the husband-wife team. We had Prateek Jain and Saloni Jain from Curious Cobo; Paresh Gupta and Pooja Gupta from My Carrer Architects; Jagruti Shringi and Vishwas Shringi from Vyolla and, Alop Mehta and Nidhi Mehta from Needy Bee speak about their experience, the pros of founding a start-up as a couple, the issues that come up, how to overcome the conflicts and so many more pearls of wisdom made of experience
